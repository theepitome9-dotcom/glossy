How to Choose the Right Home-Trades App

  • 1
    Look for AI-powered or data-backed instant quotes. A good app should generate a price range from your room dimensions and postcode — not just connect you with someone who will quote later. This saves hours of back-and-forth.
  • 2
    Check that pricing is postcode-aware. Labour costs in London can be 40–60% higher than in the North East. An app that ignores regional variation will give you a misleading estimate.
  • 3
    Verify how professionals are vetted. Look for platforms that require real profiles, trade descriptions, past-work photos, and customer reviews tied to actual completed jobs — not just self-reported credentials.
  • 4
    Confirm it covers your specific trade. Painting and decorating is a distinct skill-set from plastering, tiling, or electrical work. Choose an app that clearly separates these categories so your job reaches the right professionals.
  • 5
    Make sure it is free for homeowners. The best model charges tradespeople per lead or via subscription — not the homeowner. You should be able to estimate, post a job, and compare professionals at zero cost.
