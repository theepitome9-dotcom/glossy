How to Choose the Right Home Trades App

  • 1
    Demand an instant quote before you commit to anything. The best apps give you a realistic price range immediately — based on your actual dimensions and postcode — so you have a reliable budget figure before speaking to a single tradesperson. If an app can't do this, you're flying blind.
  • 2
    Job posting should be completely free for homeowners. You should never pay to list a job. The right model charges professionals for lead access, not you. Avoid platforms that charge customers listing fees or take a percentage of the job value.
  • 3
    Check how professionals are vetted and how leads are sold. The best platforms cap the number of professionals who receive each lead (Glossy caps at 4), meaning you get focused, serious enquiries rather than dozens of generic messages. Avoid platforms that sell your contact details to unlimited professionals.
  • 4
    Make sure the app covers your specific trade. Construction projects span many disciplines — a painting job is different from a plumbing job or kitchen fit. Use an app that cleanly separates categories so your job is seen by the right professional, not a generic all-trades directory.
  • 5
    Look for postcode-aware regional pricing. UK labour costs vary by up to 60% between regions. An instant estimate that ignores your postcode is not useful. Reliable apps apply regional rate data to every quote.
